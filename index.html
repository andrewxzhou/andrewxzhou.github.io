ABOUT ME

A little about me:
I'd describe myself as a jack-of-all trades, mostly. I like CS because of the fun problem-solving bits and because its super cool to see something you made just work when you press the run button. I'm also interested in econ and politics because of all the real-world implications they have, and because they segue nicely with history, which I also find pretty interesting.

My first exposure to CS was way back in elementary school with basic stuff like hour of code and Destination Imagination. I only really got interested, though, when I was in middle school and I took an intro course to computer science. Going from basic stuff like printing "hello world" all the way to making a full dice game in Python(with a lot of help from docs and my teacher) was one of the first times CS made me think "woah, that's cool."

During middle school, I also started getting more involved with more social sciency stuff, mainly debate. This started with me taking an intro to public speaking course because I was worried about being bad at it. But then I realized that I actually liked debate, especially because of the research part of it, which helped begin my interest in politics and econ and whatnot. This interest was further developed in 8th grade, when I had an amazing history teacher who really furthered my interest in history and politics. Then in 9th grade, I took AP HuG, which furthered my interest in current events, politics, and geography.

I'm not just an academic robot, though! My hobbies include: playing Genshin Impact, watching anime, and reading (mainly historical and manga).
